1. **Differentiate between Hazard and Risk**  
- **Hazard:** A potential source of harm or adverse effect on people, property, or environment.  
- **Risk:** The probability or likelihood that a hazard will cause damage or loss.

---

2. **Hydrological Hazard**  
A hydrological hazard is a disaster caused by the occurrence, movement, or distribution of surface or underground water.  
**Examples:** Floods, Tsunamis, Storm surges, Coastal erosion.

---

3. **Classification of Types of Disaster**  
1. **Natural Disasters:** Earthquakes, floods, cyclones, droughts, volcanic eruptions.  
2. **Man-made/Technological Disasters:** Industrial accidents, chemical spills, nuclear accidents, transport accidents.  
3. **Environmental Disasters:** Deforestation, soil erosion, desertification, water pollution.

---

4. **Earthquake Zones in India**  
India is divided into **four seismic zones** based on intensity:  
- **Zone V:** Very high-risk areas (Himalayan region, North-East).  
- **Zone IV:** High-risk areas (parts of Delhi, Punjab, and Rajasthan).  
- **Zone III:** Moderate-risk areas (Karnataka, Gujarat).  
- **Zone II:** Low-risk areas (South India, central plains).

---

5. **Meaning of Capacity Building**  
Capacity building is the process of **developing and strengthening skills, resources, and capabilities** of individuals, communities, and institutions to effectively manage disasters.

7. **Functions of State Disaster Management Authority (SDMA)**  
- Formulates **state-level disaster management policies and plans**.  
- Advises state government on **mitigation and preparedness measures**.  
- Coordinates **disaster response and relief operations**.  
- Promotes **community awareness and capacity building**.  
- Integrates **disaster risk reduction into development planning**.

---

8. **Benefits of Disaster Management Centers**  
- Efficient coordination during disasters.  
- Rapid response and relief operations.  
- Training and capacity building for officials and volunteers.  
- Hazard mapping and early warning systems.  
- Reduces loss of life, property, and environmental damage.

10. **Different Types of Satellites Used for Monitoring Environmental Hazards**  
- **Meteorological Satellites:** Monitor weather and cyclones (INSAT, NOAA).  
- **Remote Sensing Satellites:** Observe land use, flood, and drought (IRS, Landsat).  
- **Communication Satellites:** Relay disaster warnings.  
- **Earth Observation Satellites:** Track earthquakes, landslides, and forest fires.  
- **Navigation Satellites:** Assist in GPS-based rescue and relief operations.

1. **Difference Between Natural Disasters and Man-Made Disasters**  
| Aspect | Natural Disasters | Man-Made Disasters |
|--------|-----------------|------------------|
| Cause | Occur due to natural phenomena | Caused by human activities |
| Examples | Earthquakes, floods, cyclones, droughts | Industrial accidents, chemical spills, nuclear accidents |
| Predictability | Often difficult to predict precisely | Usually preventable with proper safety measures |
| Impact | Affects environment and human life | Primarily affects human health, property, and environment |

---

2. **Definitions**  
i) **Volcano:** A volcano is an opening in the Earth's surface through which magma, gases, and ash erupt.  
ii) **Tsunami:** A tsunami is a series of large ocean waves caused by underwater earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, or landslides.

---

3. **Disaster Risk Management**  
Disaster Risk Management (DRM) is the systematic process of **identifying, assessing, and reducing the risks of disasters** to minimize loss of life, property, and environment through preparedness, mitigation, response, and recovery.

---

4. **Soil Erosion**  
Soil erosion is the **removal of the top layer of soil by wind, water, or human activity**, leading to land degradation and reduced agricultural productivity.

---

5. **Short Note on Capacity Building**  
Capacity building refers to **enhancing the skills, resources, and capabilities** of individuals, communities, and institutions to effectively manage and respond to disasters. It includes training, awareness programs, institutional development, and community participation.

---

6. **Non-Structural Mitigation Measures for Disaster Risk Reduction**  
- Awareness campaigns and community education  
- Land-use planning and zoning regulations  
- Early warning systems and forecasting  
- Training and drills for emergency preparedness  
- Policies and laws for disaster management  
- Environmental conservation and ecosystem management

---

7. **Application of Counter-Disaster Resources**  
Counter-disaster resources include manpower, equipment, and materials used during disasters. Their application involves:  
- Search and rescue operations  
- Providing medical aid and first aid  
- Relief distribution (food, water, shelter)  
- Temporary evacuation centers and shelter management  
- Coordination with local and national authorities

---

8. **Uses of Survival Kits in Disaster Management**  
Survival kits provide **essential supplies for immediate survival** during disasters. They include:  
- Drinking water and non-perishable food  
- First aid and medications  
- Flashlights, batteries, and communication devices  
- Personal hygiene items  
- Blankets, clothing, and emergency tools  
**Purpose:** Supports life and basic needs until help arrives.

---

9. **Role of Emergency Stage in Disaster Management**  
The emergency stage involves **immediate actions following a disaster** to save lives and reduce suffering. Key roles:  
- Rescue operations and evacuation  
- Medical aid and first aid services  
- Providing food, water, and shelter  
- Maintaining law and order  
- Rapid damage assessment for planning recovery

---

10. **Steps in the Process of Disaster Preparedness**  
1. **Hazard and Risk Assessment:** Identify potential hazards and vulnerable areas.  
2. **Planning:** Develop emergency plans, evacuation routes, and resource allocation.  
3. **Training and Capacity Building:** Educate communities, train responders, and conduct drills.  
4. **Early Warning Systems:** Install systems for alerts and timely communication.  
5. **Resource Mobilization:** Ensure availability of emergency supplies, personnel, and equipment.  
6. **Community Participation:** Engage local communities in preparedness and mitigation measures.  
7. **Monitoring and Evaluation:** Regularly update plans and review preparedness measures.

1. **Define Disaster and Different Types of Disasters**  
- **Disaster:** A disaster is a sudden event, natural or man-made, that causes significant **loss of life, property, and disrupts normal life**.  
- **Types of Disasters:**  
  1. **Natural Disasters:** Earthquakes, floods, cyclones, droughts, landslides, volcanic eruptions.  
  2. **Man-Made Disasters:** Industrial accidents, chemical spills, nuclear accidents, transport accidents.  
  3. **Environmental Disasters:** Deforestation, soil erosion, desertification, pollution.  

---

2. **Sedimentation Problem**  
Sedimentation is the **accumulation of soil, silt, and debris in rivers, reservoirs, or canals**, reducing water carrying capacity and causing floods, waterlogging, and loss of storage.

---

3. **Disaster Risk**  
Disaster risk is the **likelihood or probability of harmful consequences** resulting from the interaction between hazards, vulnerability, and exposure of people, property, and environment.

---

4. **Components of Post-Flood Rehabilitation Measures**  
- **Restoration of Infrastructure:** Roads, bridges, water supply, electricity.  
- **Housing Reconstruction:** Repair and rebuild damaged homes.  
- **Health and Sanitation:** Prevent disease outbreaks, provide medical facilities.  
- **Livelihood Support:** Restore agriculture, employment, and income sources.  
- **Psychosocial Support:** Counseling and community rebuilding programs.  
- **Resource Distribution:** Supply of food, clean water, and clothing.

7. **Occupational Health Hazards and Its Types**  
- **Definition:** Risks and dangers arising from workplace conditions that can cause injury, illness, or long-term health problems.  
- **Types:**  
  1. **Physical Hazards:** Noise, vibration, extreme temperatures, radiation.  
  2. **Chemical Hazards:** Toxic chemicals, gases, solvents.  
  3. **Biological Hazards:** Bacteria, viruses, fungi.  
  4. **Ergonomic Hazards:** Poor posture, repetitive movements.  
  5. **Psychosocial Hazards:** Work stress, harassment.  
  6. **Mechanical Hazards:** Machinery-related injuries.  

---

8. **Risk Transfer**  
Risk transfer is the **shift of financial or operational risk from one party to another**, often through insurance, contracts, or agreements to reduce the impact of disasters.

---

9. **Define Rehabilitation**  
Rehabilitation is the **process of restoring normal life and livelihoods** of people affected by disasters, including housing, infrastructure, health, education, and economic recovery.

---

- Conducts **training and capacity building** for officials, communities, and NGOs.  
- Provides **research, studies, and technical guidance** on disaster mitigation.  
- Develops **policies and strategies** for national and state disaster management plans.  
- Promotes **awareness campaigns and knowledge dissemination**.  
- Maintains **data and knowledge repository** for effective disaster planning.  
- Collaborates with **national and international agencies** for technology and expertise transfer.
