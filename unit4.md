**19. Disaster Management Act, 2005**
(10 Marks)

**Introduction**  
The Disaster Management Act, 2005, is a landmark legislation in India that provides a legal framework for **efficient disaster management** at national, state, and district levels. It focuses on preparedness, mitigation, response, and recovery.

---

### **Key Features of the Act:**

1. **National Disaster Management Authority (NDMA)**
   - Headed by the Prime Minister.
   - Responsible for laying down policies, plans, and guidelines for disaster management.

2. **State Disaster Management Authorities (SDMAs)**
   - Headed by respective Chief Ministers.
   - Implements disaster management plans at the state level.

3. **District Disaster Management Authorities (DDMAs)**
   - Headed by District Collectors or Magistrates.
   - Executes disaster management activities locally.

4. **National Executive Committee (NEC)**
   - Assists NDMA in preparing national plans and coordinating disaster response.
   - Members include secretaries of various central ministries.

5. **Focus Areas**
   - Disaster prevention, mitigation, preparedness, response, and recovery.
   - Promotes community awareness and capacity building.
   - Integrates disaster risk reduction into development planning.

6. **Offenses and Penalties**
   - Punishment for negligence in disaster management duties.
   - Legal accountability for non-compliance with the Act.

---

**Conclusion**  
The Disaster Management Act, 2005, ensures **structured, coordinated, and accountable disaster management** in India, emphasizing preparedness and mitigation.

**20A. Role of NGOs in Disaster Management Plan**
(7 Marks)

**Introduction**  
Non-Governmental Organizations (NGOs) play a critical role in disaster management by bridging gaps between government efforts and community needs.

---

### **Roles of NGOs:**

1. **Preparedness and Awareness**
   - Conduct training, workshops, and community drills.
   - Educate people on preventive measures and emergency response.

2. **Immediate Relief and Rescue**
   - Provide food, water, shelter, and medical assistance during disasters.
   - Participate in search and rescue operations.

3. **Rehabilitation and Recovery**
   - Assist in rebuilding homes, schools, and infrastructure.
   - Support livelihood restoration and psychological counseling.

4. **Resource Mobilization**
   - Fundraising and donation collection for disaster relief.
   - Supply essential goods and equipment.

5. **Advocacy and Policy Support**
   - Raise public awareness and advocate for disaster-resilient policies.
   - Collaborate with government agencies in planning and implementation.

---

**Conclusion**  
NGOs complement government efforts, strengthen community participation, and ensure that relief reaches vulnerable populations effectively.

(10 Marks)

**National Disaster Management Authority (NDMA)**

- Formulates **national disaster management policy** and guidelines.
- Prepares **national disaster management plans**.
- Coordinates with central ministries, state authorities, and agencies.
- Promotes **mitigation, preparedness, and capacity building**.
- Provides **technical assistance and training** for disaster management.

- Assists NDMA in **preparing national plans**.
- Coordinates between **central ministries and state governments**.

**20. Factors to Consider While Planning Rebuilding Works After a Major Earthquake** 
(10 Marks)

**Introduction**  
Reconstruction after an earthquake requires careful planning to ensure **safety, resilience, and sustainability**.

---

### **Key Factors:**

1. **Structural Safety**
   - Earthquake-resistant building designs and retrofitting old structures.
   - Compliance with seismic codes and standards.

2. **Site Selection**
   - Avoiding landslide-prone, flood-prone, or unstable areas.
   - Prioritizing accessible and safe locations.

3. **Infrastructure Restoration**
   - Rebuilding essential services: water supply, electricity, hospitals, and schools.

4. **Community Participation**
   - Involving local population in planning and construction.
   - Ensures cultural appropriateness and better maintenance.

5. **Use of Local Materials**
   - Economical, readily available, and suitable for local conditions.
   - Encourages sustainable reconstruction.

6. **Economic Considerations**
   - Budget allocation, resource management, and cost-effective reconstruction methods.

7. **Disaster Preparedness Integration**
   - Incorporating early warning systems, evacuation plans, and open spaces.
   - Planning for future risk reduction.

8. **Psychosocial Support**
   - Rebuilding community spaces and providing mental health support.
   - Encouraging return to normal life.

---

**Conclusion**  
Rebuilding after an earthquake must focus on **resilience, sustainability, and preparedness**, ensuring that communities are safer and better prepared for future disasters.

**19. State Disaster Management Authority and State Disaster Management Plan** 
(10 Marks)

**State Disaster Management Authority (SDMA)**  

**Introduction**  
The SDMA is the apex body in each Indian state responsible for **disaster management** at the state level. It functions under the **guidelines of the National Disaster Management Authority (NDMA)**.

**Key Features:**
- Headed by the **Chief Minister** of the state.
- Members include state ministers and experts in disaster management.
- Coordinates with NDMA, district authorities, and other stakeholders.

**Functions of SDMA:**
1. Formulates **state-level disaster management policies and plans**.
2. Advises the **state government** on mitigation and preparedness measures.
3. Promotes **capacity building** and awareness programs for communities.
4. Coordinates **response and relief operations** during disasters.
5. Ensures integration of **disaster risk reduction** into development planning.

---

**State Disaster Management Plan (SDMP)**  

**Introduction**  
The SDMP is a comprehensive document that outlines **preparedness, mitigation, response, and recovery strategies** for disasters within the state.

**Key Components:**
- Hazard and vulnerability assessment for the state.
- Roles and responsibilities of various departments and agencies.
- Resource mapping and mobilization plan.
- Procedures for early warning, evacuation, and relief operations.
- Community participation and awareness programs.
- Guidelines for post-disaster rehabilitation and reconstruction.

**20. National Institute of Disaster Management (NIDM) and Its Functions**  
(10 Marks)

**Introduction**  
The National Institute of Disaster Management (NIDM) is an autonomous institute under the **Ministry of Home Affairs**, established to **train, educate, and research disaster management** in India.

**Functions of NIDM:**
1. **Training and Capacity Building**
   - Conducts workshops, training programs, and skill development for government officials, NGOs, and community volunteers.
2. **Research and Development**
   - Undertakes studies on hazards, vulnerabilities, and disaster management strategies.
3. **Policy and Plan Support**
   - Provides technical guidance for national and state disaster management plans.
4. **Awareness and Advocacy**
   - Promotes disaster awareness through publications, campaigns, and educational programs.
5. **Knowledge Management**
   - Maintains a repository of disaster-related information, best practices, and case studies.
6. **Collaboration**
   - Works with national and international agencies for technology transfer and capacity enhancement.

**19. District Disaster Management Authority (DDMA) and Its Functions**
(10 Marks)

**Introduction**  
The DDMA is the **apex body at the district level** responsible for disaster management planning and execution under the supervision of SDMA and NDMA.

**Key Features:**
- Headed by the **District Collector or District Magistrate**.
- Includes representatives from police, health, fire, and other key departments.
- Acts as the **coordinating authority** for disaster preparedness and response.

**Functions of DDMA:**
1. Prepares and implements **district disaster management plans**.
2. Coordinates **response, relief, and rehabilitation** during disasters.
3. Conducts **risk assessment and vulnerability mapping**.
4. Monitors **early warning systems** and community preparedness activities.
5. Ensures training and awareness programs for local communities.
6. Supervises **use of disaster funds and resources** at the district level.

**20. National Disaster Response Force (NDRF) and Their Duties** 
(10 Marks)

**Introduction**  
The National Disaster Response Force (NDRF) is a **specialized force** created under the Disaster Management Act, 2005, to respond to disasters and emergencies in India.

**Key Features:**
- Under the **Ministry of Home Affairs**.
- Composed of multiple battalions of trained personnel.
- Equipped with modern rescue and relief tools.

**Duties of NDRF:**
1. **Search and Rescue Operations**
   - Conduct operations during earthquakes, floods, cyclones, landslides, and industrial accidents.
2. **Medical Assistance**
   - Provides first aid, emergency care, and medical evacuation.
3. **Evacuation and Relief Operations**
   - Assists in safe evacuation of people from hazardous areas.
4. **Capacity Building**
   - Trains state disaster response forces, volunteers, and communities.
5. **Hazard-Specific Response**
   - Specialized units for chemical, biological, radiological, and nuclear emergencies.
6. **Collaboration**
   - Coordinates with NDMA, SDMA, DDMA, and other government agencies for effective disaster management.

**Conclusion**  
NDRF plays a **critical role in rapid response**, reducing loss of life, and supporting disaster preparedness and mitigation efforts across India.
