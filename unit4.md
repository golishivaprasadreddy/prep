17. Role of Mass Media and Communication in Disaster Management  
(10 Marks)

**Introduction**  
Mass media and communication play a crucial role in disaster management by spreading timely information, creating awareness, and assisting in response and recovery operations.

---

### **Roles of Mass Media in Disaster Management:**

1. **Early Warning and Alerts**
   - Media platforms such as radio, TV, internet, and mobile alerts warn people about impending disasters like cyclones, floods, or earthquakes.
   - This helps in timely evacuation and preparedness.

2. **Public Awareness and Education**
   - Media educates people on safety measures, first aid, and preventive actions.
   - Awareness campaigns reduce panic and guide people on what to do.

3. **Communication During Disaster**
   - Media bridges the communication gap between authorities, relief teams, and the public.
   - Helps locate missing persons and identify affected areas.

4. **Coordination and Mobilization of Resources**
   - Encourages volunteers, NGOs, and donors to support rescue efforts.
   - Shows real-time needs like food, clothing, medical assistance.

5. **Monitoring and Accountability**
   - Keeps track of relief activities and ensures transparency in government operations.
   - Reports misuse of funds or delay in aid delivery.

6. **Post-Disaster Rehabilitation Support**
   - Highlights long-term needs such as housing, education, and livelihood support.
   - Encourages rehabilitation programs and community rebuilding.

---

**Conclusion**  
Mass media plays an essential role in preparedness, response, and recovery by spreading accurate information, saving lives, and supporting coordinated relief efforts.

18. Chemical Hazards and Measures for Mitigation (Example: Bhopal Gas Tragedy)  
(10 Marks)

**Introduction**  
Chemical hazards arise from accidental release of toxic compounds during industrial operations, storage, or transportation. They can lead to severe health effects, environmental pollution, and long-term consequences.

---

### **Corrective Measures to Mitigate Chemical Hazards:**

1. **Strict Safety Regulations**
   - Enforcing industrial laws and monitoring compliance.
   - Use of safety equipment and inspection of storage systems.

2. **Emergency Preparedness Plan**
   - On-site and off-site emergency plans.
   - Regular disaster drills and employee safety training.

3. **Hazard Identification and Risk Assessment**
   - Monitoring chemical handling, pressure levels, and gas leakage points.
   - Safety audits.

4. **Early Warning and Detection Systems**
   - Gas sensors, alarms, and remote monitoring systems.

5. **Public Awareness and Community Preparedness**
   - Educating nearby residents on evacuation procedures and first aid.

---

### **Example: Bhopal Gas Disaster (1984)**

- The accident occurred at Union Carbide pesticide plant in Bhopal.
- A large quantity of **Methyl Isocyanate (MIC)** gas leaked, killing thousands instantly and affecting millions long-term.
- Lack of safety systems, poor maintenance, and absence of emergency response caused widespread disaster.

---

**Conclusion**  
Chemical hazards can be minimized through regulations, technology, preparedness, and community awareness. The Bhopal incident remains a critical lesson for improving industrial safety.

18. Industrial Safety Plan for Disaster Management  
(10 Marks)

**Introduction**  
Industrial safety planning ensures that industries operate safely and are prepared to prevent, respond to, and recover from industrial disasters.

---

### **Components of Industrial Safety Plan:**

1. **Hazard Identification and Risk Assessment**
   - Analysis of toxic chemicals, machinery, and potential failure points.
   - Workplace inspections and safety audits.

2. **Safety Policy and Regulatory Compliance**
   - Following national and international standards.
   - Regular certification and compliance checks.

3. **Emergency Preparedness Plan**
   - On-site and off-site plans with clear responsibilities.
   - Includes fire drills, evacuation maps, and emergency response teams.

4. **Safety Infrastructure**
   - Fire suppression systems, alarms, gas detectors, PPE, emergency exits.
   - Safe storage facilities and ventilation systems.

5. **Training and Capacity Building**
   - Safety training for employees, managers, and contractors.
   - First aid and firefighting training.

6. **Monitoring and Early Warning Systems**
   - Sensors for gas leakage, temperature rise, and pressure imbalance.
   - Continuous monitoring using automated control systems.

7. **Coordination with Local Authorities**
   - Collaboration with fire services, hospitals, police, and disaster teams.

8. **Post-Disaster Response and Recovery**
   - Medical support, containment of hazards, and damage assessment.

---

**Conclusion**  
A well-planned industrial safety system reduces risks, protects workers and communities, and ensures sustainable industrial operations.

17. Setting Up Relief Camps and Role of International Organizations  
(10 Marks)

**Introduction**  
Relief camps are temporary shelters established to provide immediate assistance to people affected by disasters. They aim to ensure safety, meet basic needs, and facilitate recovery.

---

### **Steps to Set Up Relief Camps:**

1. **Site Selection**
   - Choose safe, elevated, and accessible locations away from disaster-affected hazards.
   - Ensure availability of open space and transportation access.

2. **Infrastructure Setup**
   - Erect temporary shelters such as tents or prefabricated units.
   - Provide basic facilities: drinking water, toilets, sanitation, and cooking areas.

3. **Resource Allocation**
   - Supply essentials such as food, blankets, clothing, medicines, and first-aid kits.
   - Ensure proper storage and distribution mechanisms.

4. **Health and Hygiene Measures**
   - Medical teams for first aid and disease prevention.
   - Maintain clean water and sanitation to avoid outbreaks.

5. **Security and Management**
   - Deploy security personnel to maintain order.
   - Assign coordinators for logistics, volunteer management, and communication.

6. **Psychosocial Support**
   - Counseling services for trauma-affected individuals.
   - Activities for children and vulnerable populations to reduce stress.

---

### **Role of International Organizations (Example: UNDP)**

- **UNDP (United Nations Development Programme)**
  - Provides technical and financial assistance.
  - Supports governments in planning, logistics, and capacity building.
  - Offers expertise in shelter, health, water, and sanitation.

- **Success Achieved**
  - Effective post-disaster response in countries like Indonesia, Haiti, and India.
  - Rapid deployment of resources and coordination with local authorities.
  - Enhanced resilience through community training and disaster preparedness programs.

---

**Conclusion**  
Relief camps are essential for immediate post-disaster support. International organizations like UNDP enhance efficiency, coordination, and long-term recovery outcomes.

17. Occupational Health Hazards: Meaning and Types  
(10 Marks)

**Introduction**  
Occupational health hazards are risks and dangers that workers face due to workplace conditions. These hazards can lead to injury, illness, or long-term health issues.

---

### **Types of Occupational Health Hazards:**

1. **Physical Hazards**
   - Noise, vibration, extreme temperatures, radiation, poor lighting.
   - Can cause hearing loss, heat stroke, burns, or accidents.

2. **Chemical Hazards**
   - Exposure to harmful chemicals, solvents, pesticides, or gases.
   - May result in poisoning, respiratory problems, or skin disorders.

3. **Biological Hazards**
   - Bacteria, viruses, fungi, or other infectious agents.
   - Common in hospitals, labs, agriculture, and food industries.

4. **Ergonomic Hazards**
   - Poor workplace design, repetitive movements, improper posture.
   - Leads to musculoskeletal disorders and fatigue.

5. **Psychosocial Hazards**
   - Work-related stress, harassment, shift work, and high workload.
   - Can result in mental health issues and reduced productivity.

6. **Mechanical Hazards**
   - Injuries from machinery, moving parts, or equipment malfunction.
   - Includes cuts, crush injuries, and amputations.

---

**Conclusion**  
Identification and management of occupational health hazards are crucial to ensure worker safety, productivity, and well-being.

18. Coping Strategies Related to Disasters  
(10 Marks)

**Introduction**  
Coping strategies are the methods and practices adopted by individuals, communities, and organizations to manage the impact of disasters and recover effectively.

---

### **Types of Coping Strategies:**

1. **Preparedness Strategies**
   - Early warning systems, emergency kits, and evacuation plans.
   - Training and drills for individuals and communities.

2. **Mitigation Strategies**
   - Building disaster-resilient structures and infrastructure.
   - Land-use planning and environmental protection measures.

3. **Response Strategies**
   - Immediate relief such as medical aid, food distribution, and rescue operations.
   - Coordination of emergency services and volunteers.

4. **Recovery Strategies**
   - Rehabilitation of affected people, reconstruction of infrastructure.
   - Psychological counseling, livelihood support, and rebuilding communities.

5. **Adaptive Strategies**
   - Learning from previous disasters to improve future response.
   - Community participation and capacity building.

6. **Policy and Institutional Strategies**
   - Strong governance, disaster management laws, and inter-agency coordination.
   - Financial support, insurance, and resource allocation for emergencies.

---

**Conclusion**  
Effective coping strategies reduce the vulnerability of communities, enhance resilience, and ensure a structured approach to disaster preparedness, response, and recovery.
