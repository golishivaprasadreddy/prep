**15A. Structural Measures for Control of Floods** 
(5 Marks)

**Introduction**  
Structural measures are engineered systems and physical constructions designed to reduce, control, or manage floodwaters. Their main purpose is to protect life, infrastructure, and property.

**Key Structural Measures:**

1. **Dams and Reservoirs**  
   - Constructed to store excess water during heavy rainfall.  
   - Controlled release reduces downstream flooding.

2. **Levees and Embankments**  
   - Raised banks built along rivers.  
   - Prevent water from overflowing into nearby settlements.

3. **Floodwalls**  
   - Concrete or masonry structures used mainly in urban regions.  
   - Protect cities where embankments are not feasible.

4. **Channel Improvements (River Training)**  
   - Includes widening, deepening, straightening, and dredging rivers.  
   - Increases water flow capacity and prevents overflow.

5. **Stormwater Drainage Systems**  
   - Designed in metropolitan areas to manage runoff.  
   - Very important for prevention of urban flooding.

15B. Hazards Associated With Volcanic Eruptions  
(5 Marks)

**Introduction**  
Volcanic eruptions pose several life-threatening hazards to humans, wildlife, and the environment.

**Major Hazards:**

1. **Lava Flows**  
   - Molten rock burns and destroys everything in its path.

2. **Ash Fall**  
   - Causes respiratory problems, visibility issues, and roof collapse.

3. **Pyroclastic Flows**  
   - Extremely hot, fast-moving clouds of gases and debris.  
   - Causes instant destruction.

4. **Lahars (Volcanic Mudflows)**  
   - Water mixed with volcanic debris forms fast-moving mud flows.  
   - Destroys infrastructure and settlements.

5. **Toxic Gases**  
   - Emission of CO₂, SO₂, and other harmful gases affects health and environment.

6. **Volcanic Tsunamis**  
   - Triggered when volcanic material displaces ocean water.

16A. Impact of Volcanic Eruption on Environment  
(6 Marks)

**Major Environmental Impacts:**

- **Air Pollution:** Release of ash and toxic gases affects health and air quality.  
- **Climate Change:** Aerosols reflect sunlight, causing temporary global cooling.  
- **Destruction of Ecosystems:** Lava and pyroclastic materials destroy forests and wildlife.  
- **Water Contamination:** Ash mixes with water bodies making them unsafe for use.
- **Soil Fertility:** Over time, mineral-rich volcanic ash improves soil fertility.
- **Ocean Acidification:** CO₂ emissions reduce pH affecting marine life.

**Conclusion:**  
Volcanic eruptions cause immediate environmental destruction but may bring long-term ecological benefits.

(4 Marks)

**Role of GIS:**

- **Hazard Mapping & Risk Assessment:** Identifies vulnerable zones such as flood plains, fault lines, and cyclone-prone regions.
- **Real-time Monitoring:** Integrates weather data, satellite images, and sensors for early warning.
- **Resource Planning:** Optimizes evacuation routes, shelter locations, and relief distribution.
- **Damage Assessment:** Helps authorities assess impact and plan long-term recovery.

GIS supports all phases of disaster management from mitigation to recovery.

15. Various Elements of Capacity Building  
(10 Marks)

**Introduction**  
Capacity building refers to the development and strengthening of human, institutional, and community capabilities to effectively prepare, respond, and recover from disasters. It enhances resilience and reduces vulnerability.

---

### **Elements of Capacity Building:**

1. **Human Resource Development**
   - Involves training, education, and skill enhancement.
   - Includes community volunteers, emergency responders, and government officials.
   - Ensures people are equipped with knowledge on evacuation, first aid, safety, and survival.

2. **Institutional Development**
   - Strengthening policies, frameworks, and coordination among institutions.
   - Improves functioning of disaster management authorities at national, state, and district levels.
   - Helps create clear emergency roles and communication channels.

3. **Infrastructure and Technology**
   - Establishing early warning systems, resilient buildings, emergency shelters, and communication networks.
   - Usage of tools like GIS, satellites, forecasting systems, and modern equipment for rescue and relief.

4. **Financial Resource Management**
   - Includes budget allocation, insurance schemes, risk-sharing mechanisms, and dedicated funds.
   - Ensures adequate financial support for preparedness, response, and recovery.

5. **Community Participation**
   - Communities are the first responders during disasters.
   - Involves awareness campaigns, disaster drills, local committees, and self-help groups.
   - Encourages ownership and responsibility in disaster risk reduction.

---

**Conclusion**  
Effective capacity building strengthens preparedness and ensures resilient communities capable of managing disasters efficiently.

16. Disaster Contingency Action Plan in Disaster Management  
(10 Marks)

**Introduction**  
A contingency action plan is a predefined strategy to ensure timely and coordinated actions during disasters. It includes roles, responsibilities, resources, and procedures to minimize damage and support recovery.

---

### **Components of Disaster Contingency Plan:**

1. **Risk and Hazard Assessment**
   - Identification of vulnerable areas, population, and infrastructure.
   - Helps prioritize resource allocation.

2. **Resource Inventory and Mobilization**
   - Listing available resources such as equipment, emergency shelters, transportation, medical supplies.
   - Helps ensure quick deployment during emergencies.

3. **Command and Communication System**
   - Framework for coordination among government agencies, NGOs, and emergency teams.
   - Includes communication systems, early warning mechanisms, and control rooms.

4. **Evacuation and Rescue Plans**
   - Defines safe routes, shelters, relocation areas, and transportation methods.
   - Ensures smooth and safe evacuation of affected populations.

5. **Emergency Response and Relief Measures**
   - Includes search and rescue, first aid, food and water supply, and temporary shelter arrangement.
   - Special care for vulnerable groups: elderly, disabled, children, and pregnant women.

6. **Training, Mock Drills, and Awareness**
   - Regular practice improves efficiency and identifies gaps in planning.

---

**Conclusion**  
A contingency action plan ensures preparedness and improves coordination for effective disaster response and recovery.

15. Short Note on Extra-Planetary Hazards  
(10 Marks)

**Introduction**  
Extra-planetary hazards are disasters originating from outer space that may affect the Earth’s environment, life, and technological systems.

---

### **Major Extra-Planetary Hazards:**

1. **Asteroid and Meteorite Impacts**
   - Large space rocks entering Earth’s atmosphere may cause massive destruction.
   - Example: The Tunguska event (1908).

2. **Solar Flares and Geomagnetic Storms**
   - Caused by solar explosions releasing charged particles.
   - Can damage communication systems, satellites, power grids, and GPS networks.

3. **Cosmic Radiation**
   - High-energy particles from space pose risks to astronauts and aviation systems.

4. **Comet Collisions**
   - Though rare, large comets could drastically impact global climate and life forms.

5. **Gamma-Ray Bursts**
   - Rare but powerful radiation bursts that could damage the ozone layer if close enough.

---

**Conclusion**  
While rare, extra-planetary hazards require monitoring through space agencies and advanced warning systems to protect technology and life.

16. Institutions and National Centers Involved in Natural Disaster Reduction  
(10 Marks)

**Introduction**  
India has several national institutions focused on disaster mitigation, preparedness, research, and response to reduce the impact of natural disasters.

---

### **Key Institutions and Centers:**

1. **National Disaster Management Authority (NDMA)**
   - Apex body for policy formation, planning, and coordination for disaster management.
   - Promotes disaster-resistant infrastructure and community awareness.

2. **National Institute of Disaster Management (NIDM)**
   - Provides research, training, and capacity-building programs.

3. **Indian Meteorological Department (IMD)**
   - Responsible for weather forecasting and early warnings of cyclones, storms, and monsoons.

4. **Indian National Centre for Ocean Information Services (INCOIS)**
   - Issues tsunami warnings and ocean-related hazard alerts.

5. **Central Water Commission (CWC)**
   - Monitors river flows and provides flood forecasting.

6. **ISRO (Indian Space Research Organisation)**
   - Provides satellite data for monitoring droughts, floods, cyclones, and land use.

7. **National Remote Sensing Centre (NRSC)**
   - Uses satellite imagery for hazard mapping, risk assessment, and emergency response.

8. **State Disaster Management Authorities (SDMAs)**
   - Coordinate disaster management activities at state level.

---

**Conclusion**  
These institutions collectively contribute to preparedness, early warning, research, and coordinated response, significantly reducing disaster risks in India.
